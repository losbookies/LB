About The Club

Los Bookies is a vibrant book club that delves into a diverse selection of literature. We began our journey with Kurt Vonnegut's "Mother Night," a satirical yet profound commentary on morality and human nature. This led us to the hauntingly beautiful narrative about identity and ethics in Kazuo Ishiguro's "Never Let Me Go." Our exploration of dystopian themes continued with Aldous Huxley's "Brave New World," questioning societal norms and the future of humanity. The deep philosophical insights found in "The Bhagavad Gita," translated by Eknath Easwaran, seamlessly connected to the psychological thriller "Verity" by Colleen Hoover, which kept us on the edge of our seats with its twists and turns. Jennette McCurdy's raw and honest memoir "I'm Glad My Mom Died" gave us an intimate look into the struggles of fame and family dynamics. We then ventured into the realm of horror with Grady Hendrix's "The Southern Book Club's Guide to Slaying Vampires," which provided a unique blend of suspense and social commentary. Our curiosity for human psychology and ethical dilemmas was further ignited by Daniel Keyes' "Flowers for Algernon," offering a poignant look into the human mind and intelligence. We explored profound emotional landscapes in Haruki Murakami's "Norwegian Wood," where themes of love and loss resonated deeply with us. Finally, we concluded our journey with the thrilling science fiction of Andy Weir's "Project Hail Mary," which sparked our imagination with its innovative plot and scientific depth.
